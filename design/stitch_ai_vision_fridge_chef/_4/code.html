Spicy Tofu Bibimbap

A vibrant, nourishing plant-based take on the classic Korean comfort bowl. Crispy pan-fried tofu meets an array of fresh, seasoned vegetables, all brought together by a fiery, umami-rich gochujang sauce.

Pro Tip: Extra Crispy Tofu

For the best texture, freeze your tofu overnight and thaw it before pressing. This changes the structure, making it more porous so it absorbs the spicy marinade perfectly while achieving a meatier, satisfying chew when pan-fried.

1

Prepare the Tofu

After pressing the tofu to remove excess moisture, cut it into 1/2-inch cubes. Heat 1 tbsp of sesame oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the tofu in a single layer and pan-fry until golden and crispy on all sides, about 8-10 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.

2

Sauté the Vegetables

In the same skillet, add a touch more oil if needed. Sauté the carrots, zucchini, and mushrooms individually, seasoning each lightly with salt. Blanch the spinach in boiling water for 30 seconds, drain well, and toss with a drop of sesame oil and sesame seeds.

3

Assemble the Bowl

Divide the warm cooked rice between two wide bowls. Arrange the crispy tofu and prepared vegetables in neat sections over the rice. Top the center with a generous dollop of vegan gochujang sauce and sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds.
